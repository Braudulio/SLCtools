Introduction

QGIS Toolbox to classify seabed landforms on continental and island shelves.

The Seabed Landform Classification Toolset is a GIS toolbox designed to classify seabed landforms on continental and island shelf settings. The user is guided through a series of classification steps within a QGIS toolbox to classify prominent seabed features termed 'seabed landforms', which characterise the morphology of the seabed surface. Seabed landforms include reefs/banks, peaks, plains, scarps, channels and depressions. Plain areas can additionally be classified into high and low features at localised and broad scales to capture features within plain surfaces. Common variables for seabed classification are utilised, including slope, bathymetric position index and ruggedness, and a series of procedures are applied to identify reef outcrops and minimise noise. The classification approach applies a whole-seascape classification which is aimed to offer a flexible and user-friendly approach to extract key seabed features from high-resolution shelf bathymetry data.

The Seabed Landform Classification Toolset is available for download on GitHub:
https://github.com/LinklaterM/Seabed-Landforms-Classification-Toolset/

This toolset was developed using QGIS 3.38 Grenoble (QGIS Development Team, 2025) and requires SAGA Next Generation (Conrad et al., 2015), Whitebox Tools (Lindsay, 2016) and GDAL plugins (GDAL/OGR Contributors, 2025). It utilises scripts within Geomorphometry and Gradients Metrics Toolbox (Evans et al., 2014) which have been incorporated in to the toolset. The framework for the classification approach is described in Linklater et al., (2019) and Linklater et al., (2023).

The toolset was developed by the Coastal and Marine Team, New South Wales Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water, Australia. Funding was provided by New South Wales Climate Change Fund through the Coastal Management Funding Package and the Marine Estate Management Authority.
